About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Astrophysics & Computational Mathematics, Science and Engineering at Michigan State University, studying compact objects, relativistic phenomena, and the explosive deaths of massive stars. My research spans high-energy astrophysics, numerical simulations, and connecting theoretical models to observable cosmic phenomena.

Previously, I completed my master’s degree at CUNY under Dr. Ore Gottlieb at the Flatiron Institute. I explored outflows from core-collapse supernovae forming black holes using 3D GRMHD simulations. This work demonstrated that even non-spinning black holes can drive subrelativistic outflows, bridging the gap between spherical CCSNe and relativistic jets in gamma-ray bursts. This research became my master’s thesis and first first-author publication.

Outside research, I mentor students, organize outreach events, and promote equity in STEM, aiming to become a professor who advances science and fosters an inclusive community.
