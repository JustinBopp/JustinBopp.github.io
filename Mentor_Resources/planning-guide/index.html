Planning Guide

This page is the process guide: what to do first, what to track, and what usually helps students stay organized during the physics and astronomy graduate application cycle.

Start

Recommended Order

  1. Research programs, research groups, and possible advisors.
  2. Build a spreadsheet to track deadlines, fees, and requirements.
  3. Ask letter writers early and give them context for your goals.
  4. Draft your SOP and personal statement early enough to revise them well.
  5. Leave time for feedback, revisions, and final application checks.

Plan

Application Checklist

  • CV or resume
  • Unofficial transcripts and coursework notes
  • Statement of purpose / personal statement
  • Letter writers and deadline list
  • Test-score plan if any programs still require exams
  • Fee-waiver notes and application budget

Plan

Rough Timeline

  • Summer / early fall: research schools, advisors, and funding details.
  • Early fall: ask for letters and start drafting statements.
  • Mid fall: narrow the school list and revise materials.
  • Late fall: submit applications and confirm letters are in.
  • Winter / spring: prepare for interviews, visits, and offer decisions.

Plan

School List Strategy

Try to build a balanced list rather than only chasing one kind of program. Think about research fit, more than one possible advisor, application cost, location, and whether the program structure matches the kind of support you want.

Apply

How to Ask for Letters Well

  • Ask early rather than assuming a short turnaround will be fine.
  • Send your CV, draft statements, and a short note about your goals.
  • Include a clear deadline list and submission instructions.
  • Follow up politely if a deadline is approaching.

Apply

Advisor Fit Questions

  • Does this person actually work on the problems I want to study?
  • Are they likely to be taking students in the cycle I am applying for?
  • Can I identify more than one possible research mentor at this school?
  • Does the department have the support structure I would need?

Plan

Fee Waivers and Finances

Application costs add up quickly. Track fees early, look for fee-waiver options, and do not wait until the last minute to learn whether programs offer one.

Watch For

Common Mistakes

  • Waiting too long to ask for letters.
  • Writing statements that stay vague instead of showing research direction.
  • Applying without checking advisor fit or program structure.
  • Forgetting to track fees, deadlines, or required materials in one place.

Next Steps

After You Submit

  • Keep copies of your materials and your deadline spreadsheet.
  • Prepare for interviews or visit weekends if programs use them.
  • Compare offers by research fit, support, funding, and environment.
  • Ask current students questions when you have the chance.

Note

Who This Fits Best

This guide is mainly tuned to physics and astronomy applications. The general structure still applies more broadly, but some field-specific details may be different elsewhere.
