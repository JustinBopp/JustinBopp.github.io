Mentor Resources

Graduate School Application

This page is for students I mentor who are applying to graduate programs in physics and astronomy. It collects the resources I most often send people when they are getting started.

Quick Note

These are resources I found useful while applying to and mentoring students in physics and astronomy, but they may not fit every field equally well.

Start Here

If you are early in the process, I would start with the broad graduate application guide, then look at the essay resources, and finally use the spreadsheets and sample documents to organize your own application cycle.

  • Research programs and possible advisors
  • Contact letter writers and potential advisors
  • Draft your personal statement / SOP
  • Build a spreadsheet to track deadlines, fees, and requirements
  • Revise materials early and ask for feedback

Choosing a Path

  • Apply now if your materials are reasonably strong and you already have the research, coursework, and letters needed for the programs you want.
  • Consider a bridge program if you want more structured academic preparation, additional mentoring, or a stronger transition into a PhD environment.
  • Consider a post-bacc if you want more research experience, time to improve letters, or a chance to sharpen your research direction before applying again.

Bridge Programs

Bridge programs can be a good fit if you want more coursework, research experience, or mentoring support before starting a PhD.

They can be especially helpful if you want a more structured transition into graduate-level work or if you feel your current record does not fully reflect your potential.

Post-Bacc Positions

Post-bacc positions can be useful if you want more research time, stronger letters, or a clearer sense of what kind of graduate program fits you best.

They are often a good choice if what you need most is hands-on research experience rather than additional coursework.

  • CRESST II Post-Baccalaureate Research Positions
  • NASA PEER
  • CfA SPARK
  • KIPAC Post-Baccalaureate Fellowship
  • PAARE / Bridge Opportunities
  • EAS Job Board

FAQ

  • Do I need to contact advisors before applying? It depends on the field and program, but reaching out can be useful if you have a genuine research fit question.
  • How many schools should I apply to? There is no single number, but it helps to have a balanced list with a realistic spread of programs and advisors.
  • When should I ask for letters? Earlier than you think. Give letter writers time, context, and a clear deadline list.
