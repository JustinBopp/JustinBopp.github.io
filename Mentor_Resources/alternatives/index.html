Alternatives

Not everyone needs to apply right away. This page is for bridge programs, post-bacc options, and other paths that can make the next application cycle stronger.

Decision

Choosing a Path

  • Apply now if your materials are reasonably strong and you already have the research, coursework, and letters needed for the programs you want.
  • Consider a bridge program if you want more structured academic preparation, additional mentoring, or a stronger transition into a PhD environment.
  • Consider a post-bacc if you want more research experience, time to improve letters, or a chance to sharpen your research direction before applying again.

Bridge Programs

Bridge programs can be a good fit if you want more coursework, research experience, or mentoring support before starting a PhD.

Post-Bacc Positions

Post-bacc positions are often most useful when what you need is more hands-on research experience, stronger letters, or more time to sharpen your direction.

  • CRESST II Post-Baccalaureate Research Positions
  • NASA PEER
  • CfA SPARK
  • KIPAC Post-Baccalaureate Fellowship
  • PAARE / Bridge Opportunities

Note

How I Would Usually Think About It

If the main gap is coursework or transition support, I would look hard at bridge programs. If the main gap is research depth or letters, a post-bacc often makes more sense.
